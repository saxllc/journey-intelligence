A NACH (National Automated Clearing House) mandate is a formal authorization you give to a bank or financial institution to automatically debit or credit a specific amount from your account at regular intervals. It is a centralized system developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) to replace the older Electronic Clearing Service (ECS).

How It Works
One-Time Registration: You sign a physical form or complete a digital e-Mandate process using your net banking or debit card details.
Unique Tracking: Every successful registration generates a Unique Mandate Reference Number (UMRN), which is used to track, modify, or cancel the mandate.
Automatic Execution: Once active, the bank automatically processes the transaction on the scheduled date without requiring manual intervention for each payment.

In the UPI ecosystem, the equivalent of a NACH mandate is called UPI AutoPay. While both systems automate recurring payments, UPI AutoPay is a mobile-first feature designed for instant setup and real-time control.

How UPI AutoPay Works
Instant Setup: You initiate the mandate from a merchant's app (like Netflix or a utility provider) and authorize it immediately using your UPI PIN. Unlike NACH, which can take 3–7 days to activate, UPI mandates are live instantly.
One-Time Authorization: You enter your PIN once to set up the recurring instruction.
Transaction Limits:
Up to ₹15,000: Payments are auto-debited without requiring your PIN again.
Above ₹15,000: For higher amounts (up to ₹1 Lakh for specific categories like insurance or mutual funds), you must manually authorize each debit with your PIN for added security.
Real-Time Alerts: You receive a notification 24 hours before any scheduled debit.

UPI AutoPay: Technical & Operational Specification (2026)
This document outlines the production standards and lifecycle for UPI AutoPay (e-mandates) as governed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI).
1. Core Infrastructure & Limits
UPI AutoPay utilizes a split-tier limit system to balance user convenience with security.
Tier	Transaction Limit	PIN Requirement for Subsequent Debits
Standard	Up to ₹15,000	Not Required
Enhanced	Up to ₹1,00,000	Not Required (Specific Categories Only)
High Value	Above ₹1,00,000	Mandatory PIN for each transaction
Enhanced Limit Categories (Non-AFA):
Insurance Premiums (Life, Health, General)
Mutual Fund SIPs
Credit Card Bill Payments
Healthcare & Education Fees
2. Mandate Lifecycle Management
The system is designed for complete user autonomy through any UPI-enabled application.
Creation: Initiated via Intent (app-to-app), Collect (VPA-based), or QR code scan.
Authorization: Every new mandate requires a one-time UPI PIN entry as a digital signature.
Pre-Debit Notification: Banks/Merchants must send an alert 24 hours before the scheduled debit.
Modification/Pause: Users can "Pause" a mandate for a specific duration or modify the maximum amount limit within their UPI app.
Revocation: Users can cancel any mandate instantly; this does not require merchant approval.
3. Integration Flows for Merchants
Merchants can implement three primary flows depending on their platform:
Intent Flow: Best for mobile apps. The merchant app triggers a direct switch to the user's UPI app (e.g., Google Pay, PhonePe) with pre-filled details.
Collect Flow: Ideal for desktop/web. The merchant sends a request to the user's VPA, triggering a push notification on their phone.
QR Flow: For offline or print media. Scanning a static/dynamic QR code opens the mandate setup screen immediately.
4. 2026 Compliance Updates
Key regulatory changes effective for the 2025-2026 cycle:
Mandate Portability: Users can now view and manage mandates created on one app (e.g., Paytm) through another app (e.g., BHIM).
Non-Peak Execution: To reduce system congestion, recurring debits are encouraged during off-peak hours (mid-day or late night).
Unique Merchant Identifier (MIC): Payee PSPs must use unique MICs to prevent duplicate or fraudulent mandate registrations.
5. Management Guide (In-App)
To manage your mandates in apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm:
Open the app and tap your Profile Picture.
Select AutoPay, Mandates, or Recurring Payments.
Choose an active mandate to view its history, Pause it, or Cancel it.
Enter your UPI PIN to confirm any changes.

UPI AutoPay: Comprehensive Reference Document (2026)
This document provides a production-level technical and operational overview of UPI AutoPay (e-mandates) as governed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI).
1. Operational Standards & Limits
UPI AutoPay facilitates recurring payments with a tiered authorization model.
Transaction Caps:
Standard Recurring Payments: Up to ₹15,000 per transaction (No UPI PIN required for subsequent debits).
Enhanced Categories: Up to ₹1,00,000 for select verified industries like Insurance Premiums, Mutual Fund SIPs, and Credit Card Bills (No UPI PIN required for subsequent debits).
High-Value Transfers: Above ₹1,00,000 (Requires manual PIN authorization for every transaction).
New 2025-2026 Execution Rules:
Non-Peak Execution: NPCI now schedules bulk recurring debits during off-peak hours to ensure system stability.
Retry Caps: Background mandate execution is now subject to specific retry limits to prevent excessive server load.
2. Mandate Lifecycle Management
Users retain full control over their mandates through any UPI-enabled application like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.
Registration: Initiated via app-to-app intent, VPA collect requests, or QR code scanning.
Notification: Mandatory Pre-debit SMS/Notification must be sent to the customer at least 24 hours before the funds are deducted.
Active Management: Users can Pause, Modify, or Revoke mandates instantly via the "AutoPay" or "Mandates" section of their UPI app.
Expiry: Mandates automatically expire on the end date set during registration.
3. Common Error & Failure Codes
When a mandate registration or execution fails, the system returns specific codes. Understanding these is critical for troubleshooting.
Business/User Errors
Code	Reason	Description
Z9	Insufficient Funds	The account balance is lower than the mandate amount.
ZM / Z6	Invalid PIN / Tries Exceeded	Incorrect UPI PIN entered or too many failed attempts.
Z7	Frequency Limit Exceeded	User has exceeded the number of transactions allowed in a set period.
Z8	Transaction Limit Exceeded	The single transaction amount exceeds the limit set by the remitting bank.
QA	Mandate Paused	The user has manually paused the mandate in their UPI app.
QC / VA	Mandate Revoked	The mandate was cancelled by the user and is no longer active.
QD	Mandate Expired	The execution date is beyond the mandate's validity period.
YE / YF	Account Blocked/Frozen	The remitting or beneficiary account is restricted by the bank.
Technical & System Errors
Code	Reason	Description
U28	Bank Server Down	The remitter's bank server is unavailable or timed out.
UP / BT	PSP Timeout	The Payment Service Provider (app) did not respond in time.
U30	Debit Failed	A general failure at the remitting bank's end during the debit process.
500	Internal Server Error	Generic failure within the NPCI or bank core infrastructure.
4. Security & Compliance
Mandate Portability: Mandates are linked to the underlying bank account, allowing users to view and manage them across different UPI apps.
Aadhaar Integration: Some mandates may require Aadhaar-based authentication if bank KYC is pending (Codes AP07, AP51).
Cool-down Period: New UPI accounts or re-registered users may face a Freeze Period (Code FP) where high-value mandates cannot be created for a few hours.
