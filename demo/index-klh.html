01 — The Comprehension Gap

Enrolled but frozen.

India's DPI stack moves at protocol speed. 20 billion UPI transactions/month. 1.4 billion Aadhaar enrollments. DPI@2047 projects 4.2% GDP contribution by 2030. Yet ISIDE 2025 exposes the ground truth: India ranks 3rd globally on aggregate digitalisation but 28th per-user. Five aspirational states haven't finished DPI 1.0.

The gap isn't access. It's comprehension. Digital Procedural Lexicon — eKYC, NACH mandates, collect requests, tokenization, auto-debit — these terms carry legal and financial consequence. They're thrust upon users who cannot parse them. "Irrevocable" on an eKYC consent screen means your identity is permanently shared. Most users scroll past it.

02 — Multilingual × Multimodal × Multimodel

Not a language switcher.
A comprehension engine.

The internet's answer to language is a drop-down. English → Hindi. The dense lexicon — "irrevocable", "auto-debit mandate" — gets transliterated: अपरिवर्तनीय is no clearer than "irrevocable" to a farmer in Vidarbha. Standard ML maps words 1:1. LLMs abstract semantically — they explain consequence in spoken register. But text alone doesn't cut cognitive load.

Multimodal — not as input capture, but as output engagement. See the screen. Listen to the explanation. Conduct a conversation. "Kya Likha Hai" is the aural complement to visual UI. Multimodel — a frontier LLM (Claude) reasons in English where DPL is native; a sovereign model (Sarvam) regenerates in the user's language with culturally native delivery. Best of both. One-fourth the cost.

05 — Why Sarvam

Intelligence from the West.
Voice from the soil.

Frontier LLMs are 95% English-trained. Indian languages represent a decimal fraction of training corpus. A sovereign model trained natively on Indian languages doesn't just translate better — it speaks like your neighbor, not a Silicon Valley assistant. Sarvam's Bulbul v2 TTS delivers in voices that are culturally native across Hindi, Kannada, Tamil, and 10+ Indic languages.

The architecture: Claude reasons in English where Digital Procedural Lexicon is native. Sarvam localizes and voices in the user's language with contextually appropriate delivery. Comprehension requires both intelligence and intimacy. One provides the reasoning. The other provides the voice.
